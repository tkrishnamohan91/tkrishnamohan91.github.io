About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Korea Institute of Science and Technology with expertise in computational materials design using first-principles density functional theory (DFT) calculations combined with advanced statistical methods such as cluster expansion and machine learning approaches.

I specialize in modeling multicomponent alloys, with particular expertise in developing efficient algorithms for constructing cluster expansion models for complex materials systems. My work encompasses the computational design of novel materials for energy applications, including hydrogen storage media, corrosion-resistant alloys, and ferroelectric materials.
